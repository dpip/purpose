Noteables
A "negativity-free" social media app based on shared memories
Noteables is a "negativity-free" social media app aimed at curating important memories and evoking a feeling of nostalgia for its users.
Having held a product definition workshop with Noteabales, we knew that they were looking to emphasize their "MemorySpark" feature. We also knew that their onboarding experience and basic look and feel needed major updates.

Onboarding
Before
Noteables’ initial onboarding sequence was too long and did not evoke feelings of nostalgia. Users were told to connect their other social accounts, but not allowed to do so within the sequence itself.
After
We replaced the moose with photos that would connect emotionally with users. We focused the copy and reduced the sequence from 5 screens to 3, while still explaining what makes Noteables unique. We also integrated connecting to social media within the flow itself.
Primary Screens
Before
The original interface contained redundant actions and complicated nested tabs, making it confusing and difficult to navigate.
After
We refreshed the visual design with a lighter color palette and emphasis on images. We reorganized the screens to make “Activity” the default tab and overhauled the Memory Spark interface.
Adding a Note/Memory
Before
Users initially launched “New Note!” from the nav, which made it unclear where the note would actually appear once created. Within the new note interface, there was not a good hierarchy of information and it was unclear that elements such as date and location were intractable.
After
We put “Add a Memory” in context on both the “Activity” and “My Life” pages so that users would see their note as soon as it was added. We used inline editing on the note interface, opened up the space for writing, and divided the screen into text on top, pictures on bottom.
Results
With many universities and other organizations wanting to promote a stronger connection with their alumni, we believe Noteables is well-positioned to make a name for themselves in the already-crowded social media space.
